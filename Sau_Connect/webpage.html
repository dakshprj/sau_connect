Wildlife protection involves conserving animals, plants, and their natural habitats to maintain ecological balance. It helps prevent species extinction, preserves biodiversity, and ensures that ecosystems remain healthy and sustainable for future generations. Human activities like deforestation, poaching, and pollution make wildlife protection essential for the survival of many species.

Services2

Aquatic wildlife protection focuses on conserving animals and plants that live in water, such as fish, amphibians, marine mammals, and aquatic plants. Protecting these species involves preserving their natural habitats, preventing water pollution, controlling overfishing, and reducing human disturbances. Safeguarding aquatic ecosystems is essential for maintaining biodiversity, supporting fisheries, and ensuring the health of rivers, lakes, and oceans for future generations.

Air pollution harms birds by contaminating the air they breathe and the food they eat. Smoke, dust, and harmful gases can damage their respiratory systems and reduce their survival. Protecting birds means keeping the air clean, reducing emissions, and preserving natural habitats so they can live safely and thrive.
